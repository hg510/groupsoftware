About Us

Shaping the future of the climate, one campus at a time

Bees are crucial pollinators, transferring pollen between plants for reproduction, essential for food production. Without them, ecosystems would suffer and food production would decline.

Now come along, steve the bee is going to be showing us the significance of his community to the world at large!

The University of Exeter Botanic Garden, located on Streatham Campus, houses an 18-acre collection of plants, including rare and endangered species. It serves as a research facility and recreational space for students, staff, and the local community.

The game uses flowers from the garden to translate culture and demonstrate environmental efforts, aiming to showcase the university's commitment to sustainability.
